The Chernobyl accident: The nuclear tragedy that changed the world

On April 26, 1986, one of the biggest nuclear catastrophes in history shook the world: Reactor Number 4 of the Chernobyl nuclear power plant, located in Ukraine, then part of the Soviet Union, exploded during a safety test. This tragic incident released a massive amount of radiation into the environment, severely affecting not only the nearby region, but countries throughout Europe and beyond.

Consequences of the Accident

The explosion at Chernobyl released radiation 400 times greater than that of Hiroshima, contaminating much of Europe. The nearby city of Pripyat was evacuated, becoming a ghost town. More than 100,000 people were relocated, and the health impact was devastating: thousands of workers, known as “liquidators,” suffered serious illnesses such as cancer. The disaster also hastened the fall of the Soviet Union and changed the world's perception of nuclear power, generating new regulations and a strong anti-nuclear movement.
